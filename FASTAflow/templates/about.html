Understanding FASTA Files

The universal format for sequence data

FASTA: simplicity meets functionality

FASTA files are an essential piece of bioinformatics, providing a simple yet powerful format for representing nucleotide or peptide sequences. Developed in the early days if the of DNA sequencing world, they are still a go-to format till this day. Part of this is the simplicity of the format.

What is a fasta file

A FASTA file is a text-based format for representing nucleotide sequences (DNA or RNA) or peptide sequences. Each sequence starts with a header, a single-line description, followed by the lines of sequence data. Each header starts with a greater-than (>) symbol to differentiate the sequence from the header, and consists of a unique identifier and optionally some details about the sequence. The sequence consists of nucleotides or amino acids depending on the type of sequence.

Why FASTA

One of the main advantages of FASTA files, their simplicity, is also one of the main reasons why they have become a universal standard in the bioinformatics world. And by being that universal standard FASTA files play a crucial role in storing and sharing sequence data despite the emergence of newer formats for specific applications.
